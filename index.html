A short introduction

In 711 a large muslim army invaded the Iberian peninsula, dominated by Visigothic Kingdom. Just 2 months after, a large battle between the muslim army and the visigothic army happened. The visigothic army was defeated, including the King of the visigothics, Rodrigo, who was killed.

After that, the muslims dominated and created a great culture in the south of Spain, which survived eight centuries, from 711 to 1492.

Alcazaba of Málaga

This fortress palace, whose name in Arabic means citadel, is one of the city's historical monuments and is much visited because of its history and beauty. According to Arab Historicians, it was built between 1056 to 1062 by the King Badis, of the berber taifa of Granada.

It was built with materials like capitals and roman columns, to the nearby Roman theathre.

It is believed that the Alcazaba was a very important construction of muslim's age of Spain.

It was restored several times and most recently in the 20th century, and today the building and its important archaeological legacy can be visited.

Alcazaba of Almería

In 955, Almería gained the title of medina ("city") by the Caliph of Cordoba, Abd ar-Rahman III, and decided to start the construction, locating it in the upper sector of the city, which began in this period.

The alcazaba, provided not only with walls and towers but also with squares, houses and a mosque, was to be also the seat of the local government, commanding the city and the sea nearby.

Its intention was to protect the area's largest city at the time, Pechina.

Alhambra of Granada

The Alhambra (The Red One in arabic) is a palace and a fortress complex located in Granada. The King Muhammad I Ibn al-Ahmar, the first Nasrid Emir begun its construction.

The palace is decorated with geometric topics and a lot of verses of poems in arabic. The Alhambra is known to be the maximum product of Al-Andalus culture.

The Catholic Kings conquered the last muslim kingdom in 1492, and stablished the Alhambra as their royal palace.
